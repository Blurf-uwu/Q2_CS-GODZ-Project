Water Insecurity

Causes

  • Droughts and Climate Change: Drought, exacerbated by climate change, is a major driver of water scarcity. Rising global temperatures alter rainfall patterns, leading to increased evaporation and more severe heat waves. Climate change impacts groundwater storage and distribution, affecting billions of people, with wet regions getting wetter and dry regions drier.
2
3
  • Water Pollution: Contaminated water from sources like untreated wastewater, agricultural runoff, and industrial pollution threatens water resources. Over 80% of the world's wastewater returns to the environment untreated, diminishing freshwater availability.
  • Poor Water Management and Growing Demand: The world's growing population, urbanization, and increased freshwater demand contribute to water shortages. Cities like Cape Town and regions in China face water crises due to extreme drought, poor water management, and overconsumption.
6

Effects

  • Increases in Food Prices: Shortages in food commodities result in increased prices, limiting people's access to essential items like food, care, and healthcare.
2
3
  • Violent Conflict: Food insecurity, particularly driven by rising food prices, can contribute to violent conflicts when coupled with other political or economic factors.
  • Rise in Health-related Costs: Food insecurity increases the risk of chronic illnesses, placing a burden on healthcare systems and resulting in higher medical costs for affected households.

Food Insecurity

Causes

  • Lack of Access to Farming Lands: Many individuals lack access to farmable land, hindering their ability to produce food.
2
3
  • Land Grabbing: Large investors or government officials taking over traditionally owned land for expansion, resource extraction, or export-oriented farming contributes to food insecurity.
  • Fast-paced Population Growth: The increasing global population puts additional pressure on food resources, exacerbating food insecurity.
